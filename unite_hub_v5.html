How to Get People to Show Up
  • 1
    Lead with what's free. Put it in the subject line.
  • 2
    Scarcity framing. "Only 12 spots" beats open-ended invites.
  • 3
    Knock on doors. Personal invite beats any email.
  • 4
    Post photos after events. Builds FOMO for the next one.
  • 5
    Wednesday evenings / Sunday afternoons. Highest turnout windows.
  • 6
    Display winning work. Named credit on walls creates buzz.

📖 How This Planner Works

Your data saves locally — all changes you make are stored in this browser automatically. If you clear browser data or switch computers, you'll start fresh.

To get your own copy — download this HTML file and open it on your computer. Your version will save independently from anyone else's.

To share with your team — use Export (above) to save your building's data as a JSON file. Send the JSON + this HTML file to your colleague. They can Import it on their machine.

To back up — export your data regularly, especially before clearing browser history or switching devices.

💬 Teams & SharePoint Usage

This is a standalone HTML tool, not a live shared app. Uploading it to Teams or SharePoint does not sync edits between users.

Recommended workflow:

1. Download the planner to your computer

2. Open it locally in Chrome — this is where your edits save

3. When you need to share updates, export your data and send the JSON file via Teams

4. Your colleague imports the JSON into their own local copy

Future roadmap: This planner is designed as a foundation that could be developed into a full web app with shared access, real-time sync, and multi-building dashboards. The current version proves the concept and workflow.
